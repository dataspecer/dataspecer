Reusable vocabularies

Define new classes, properties and their relationships as well as their relationships to exisitng vocabularies as semantic data specifications, fostering semantic data interoperability. Dataspecer provides a lightweight editor efficiently supporting the most common needs when creating RDF Schema based vocabularies.

Application profiles for narrower contexts

Combine terms from existing vocabularies and adjust them to your specific context, without breaking their original semantics. Dataspecer uses the Data Specification Vocabulary (DSV) to represent context-specific adjustments to existing classes and properties in compliance with the SEMIC Style Guide.

Semantically compliant technical artifacts

Use your domain ontology or vocabulary to semi-automatically derive semantically compliant data schemas for different data formats such as XML, JSON and CSV and let Dataspecer guide you in the process, fostering technical data interoperability.

Used for Czech national data specifications

The tool is currently in use by the Czech Digital and Information Agency for management of several national data specifications, e.g. for electronic document metadata in XML (in Czech), or the datasets of the Czech Registry of Rights and Duties in RDF and JSON-LD (in Czech).

Developed at Charles University and the Czech Digital and Information Agency

We are a group of researchers/developers at the Faculty of Mathematics and Physics, Charles University, and at the department of Data Management of the Czech Digital and Information Agency, who are involved in the development of the tool.
